Invoice Automation
Workflow

End-to-end — from email thread to client invoice

01
Trigger
Email Moved to Invoice Folder
Constantine moves a relevant project thread into a dedicated Gmail folder. This single action kicks off the entire automation — no forms, no buttons, no extra steps.
Gmail n8n Gmail Node
02
Fetch
Full Thread Retrieval
The automation pulls the complete email thread — not just the triggering message. Full context is needed: agreed prices, project scope, and all client back-and-forth.
Gmail API n8n
03
Extract
AI Data Extraction
The thread is sent to an AI model with a structured prompt. It returns clean, structured data: client name, project name, line items, amounts, due date, and any relevant notes.
Claude API n8n HTTP Node
⚠ Key risk — extraction quality depends on email consistency
04
Draft
QuickBooks Invoice Created
Extracted data is matched to an existing QB customer and a draft invoice is generated automatically. If no matching client is found, the workflow flags it rather than proceeding with bad data.
QuickBooks API n8n
⚠ Key risk — client matching must be accurate
05
Approve
Constantine Reviews & Approves
An approval email is sent with a summary of the drafted invoice and a direct link to review it in QuickBooks. One reply or click confirms it's ready to send.
Gmail QuickBooks
06
Send
Invoice Delivered to Client
On approval, QuickBooks sends the finalized invoice directly to the client. The completed job is logged automatically for internal tracking and records.
QuickBooks Google Sheets
💡
Note: Steps 3 and 4 are where we'll spend the most testing time. The goal is extraction accurate enough that your review is a 30-second confirmation — not a correction session.
